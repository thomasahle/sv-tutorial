SVA provides four operators for composing two sequences:

  • s1 and s2 — both must match; they start together but may end at different cycles
  • s1 or s2 — at least one must match
  • s1 intersect s2 — both must match and end at exactly the same cycle
  • s1 within s2 — s1 starts and ends entirely inside s2's window

Open bus_check.sv and complete two properties:

  1. p_transfer using intersect: once valid is asserted, both valid and ready must hold high for exactly 4 consecutive cycles in lock-step.
  2. p_complete using and: after valid, ready must arrive within 4 cycles and done must arrive within 8 cycles — checked independently, they may complete at different times.

The key difference: intersect requires both subsequences to end at the same cycle; and ends when the longer of the two completes. Use intersect for simultaneous lock-step protocols; use and when two independent conditions must both eventually be satisfied.
